Sơn Tùng M-TP's Timeline

Follow Sơn Tùng M-TP’s evolution as his music grows, breaks records, and captures millions along the way.

  • August 5, 2011

    Cơn Mưa Ngang Qua
    (The Rain Passed By)

    44M views | 5M streams

    Cơn Mưa Ngang Qua was Son Tung's debut single, first version was shared on the online music site Zing MP3 on August 5, 2011.

    Son Tung originally wrote the song Cơn mưa ngang qua for the groups Over Band and Young Pilots before deciding to record it himself.

  • October 10, 2013

    Nắng Ấm Xa Dần
    (The Warm Sun is Fading)

    46M views | 12M streams

    Nắng Ấm Xa Dần, composed with background music sampled from As One’s Monologue and uploaded to Zing MP3 after the success of his debut single Cơn Mưa Ngang Qua.

    The track quickly gained popularity, surpassing 300,000 views on its first day, later released as part of a double single with Em của ngày hôm qua.

  • January 19, 2014

    Em Của Ngày Hôm Qua
    (You of Yesterday)

    7M views | 3.8M streams

    Em của ngày hôm qua was inspired by Son Tung M-TP’s creative vision and quickly became a phenomenon in Vietnamese music, gaining over 300,000 listens on Zing MP3 within its first day and surpassing 1 million after three days.

    The song’s video went on to reach nearly 120 million views on YouTube and topped the Zing MP3 chart for four consecutive weeks.

  • January 30, 2015

    Chắc Ai Đó Sẽ Về
    (Someone Will Return)

    56M views | 17M streams

    Released as part of the soundtrack for Chàng Trai Năm Ấy (Dandelion), Chắc Ai Đó Sẽ Cề quickly reaching 8 million listens on its first day and topping major Vietnamese music charts in its first week.

  • August 21, 2015

    Âm Thầm Bên Em
    (Silently By Your Side)

    169M views | 52M streams

    Though its commercial success was modest compared to earlier hits, Âm Thầm Bên Em became a turning point in his career, earning praise for professionalism and winning the Làn Sóng Xanh Award for Song of the Year.

  • December 2, 2015

    Buông Đôi Tay Nhau Ra
    (Let Go of Each Other's Hands)

    164M views | 36M streams

    Buông đôi tay nhau ra quickly gained attention with its audio version reaching five million views in a single day, followed by the release of its music video.

    The song was performed at major events including the 2015 Làn Sóng Xanh Awards and Hòa âm Ánh sáng (Harmony of Light) season 2 , and earned a nomination for the 2016 Giải Thưởng Cống Hiến (Dedication Music Awards).

  • May 1, 2016

    Như Ngày Hôm Qua
    (Like Yesterday)

    1M views | 14M streams

    Như Ngày Hôm Qua is a heartfelt ballad dedicated to his fans, expressing gratitude, friendship, and encouragement to stay strong through challenges.

    This song later became associated as the main theme of Son Tung’s fandom, Sky.

  • August 2, 2016

    Chúng Ta Không Thuộc Về Nhau
    (We Don't Belong Together)

    240M views | 21M streams

    Chúng Ta Không Thuộc Về Nhau quickly became one of Vietnam’s most popular songs of 2016, amassing over 100 million YouTube views and 35 million audio streams within six months.

    It was also Son Tung’s final release before leaving WePro Entertainment and manager Nguyễn Quang Huy.

  • December 31, 2016

    Lạc Trôi
    (Mindlessly Floating)

    282M views | 17M streams

    Lạc Trôi was Son Tung’s first project after leaving WePro Entertainment.

    Within two months, the MV surpassed 200 million views, becoming the first Vietnamese EDM track to reach that milestone and earning a nomination for “Music Video of the Year” at the 2018 Giải Thưởng Cống Hiến (Dedication Music Awards).

  • February 13, 2017

    Nơi Này Có Anh
    (Here With Me)

    424M views | 42M streams

    Currently his highest viewed song, Nơi này có anh's release was timed for Valentine’s Day as a tribute to his fans.

    The MV broke records with 9 million views in 24 hours, surpassed 100 million in two months as one of the fastest Asian MVs to reach that milestone, hit 200 million by 2019 as his first MV to do so, and re-entered the Hot 14 chart at number 7 in 2020.

  • May 11, 2018

    Chạy Ngay Đi
    (Run Now)

    163M views | 15M streams

    Within 24 hours, Chạy Ngay Đi reached 22 million views, becoming the most viewed song globally on YouTube at the time, topping trends in Vietnam and Japan, and appearing across multiple international charts.

    The track’s impact earned a nomination for “Music Video of the Year” at the 2019 Giải Thưởng Cống Hiến (Dedication Music Awards).

  • July 1, 2019

    Hãy Trao Cho Anh
    (Give It to Me)

    302M views | 33M streams

    Hãy Trao Cho Anh set massive records with over 25 million views in 24 hours, rapid milestones within minutes of release, cementing its status as one of his most successful songs.

    Its impact earned three nominations at the 15th Giải Thưởng Cống Hiến (Dedication Music Awards), including “Music Video of the Year,” “Song of the Year,” and “Singer of the Year.”

  • July 5, 2020

    Có Chắc Yêu Là Đây
    (For Sure This is Love)

    187M views | 38M streams

    Despite mixed reviews, Có Chắc Yêu Là Đây topped Vietnam’s iTunes chart and peaked at #5 on the Hot 14 chart.

    The MV set records as the 4th most viewed YouTube premiere worldwide with over 904,000 concurrent viewers, quickly reaching #1 trending in Vietnam and appearing across global charts.

  • December 20, 2020

    Chúng Ta Của Hiện Tại
    (Us of the Present)

    114M views | 70M streams

    Chúng Ta Của Hiện Tại, lead single from Son Tung M‑TP’s EP Chúng Ta, quickly set records reaching 1.5 million premiere views on YouTube and topping global trending charts.

    It debuted at #1 on Billboard’s LyricFind Global Chart, Billboard Vietnam’s Hot 100 Top Vietnamese Songs, and the MV won “Music Video of the Year” at the 2020 WeChoice Awards.

  • April 29, 2021

    Muộn Rồi Mà Sao Còn
    (It's Late, Why Still)

    230M views | 60M streams

    Produced and arranged by Son Tung himself, Muộn Rồi Mà Sao Còn debuted at #126 on the Billboard Global 200 Excl. US chart, making him the first Southeast Asian artist to enter it.

    In Vietnam, it reached #6 on the Billboard Top Vietnamese Songs and #8 on the Billboard Hot 100, cementing its impact as one of his most notable releases of 2021.

  • April 28, 2022

    There's No One at All

    4M views (Removed) | 12M streams

    Son Tung's first English song, ranking #1 on Billboard Vietnam Hot 100, quickly sparked controversy for its imagery. Authorities deemed harmful to social psychology, leading to its removal from YouTube Vietnam.

    M-TP Entertainment was fined and ordered to withdraw the recording, marking one of the most legally contentious moments in Son Tung’s career.

  • July 5, 2022

    There's No One at All Another Version

    25M views | 4M streams

    This reworked edition replaced the darker imagery of the original MV with a softer, more emotional portrayal of loneliness, allowing the song to be received more positively by fans.

    Though it helped restore some fan reception, it did not replicate the explosive debut of the original.

  • May 4, 2023

    Making My Way

    51M views | 16M streams

    Making My Way debuted with over 1 million Spotify streams, topped major digital platforms in Vietnam, and quickly rose to #1 on both the Billboard Vietnam Hot 100 and Top Vietnamese Songs, becoming his second chart‑topper.

    However, it was criticized for weak mixing and pronunciation issues, reflecting both ambition and controversy in his push toward international markets.

  • March 7, 2024

    Chúng Ta Của Tương Lai
    (Us of the Future)

    85M views | 38M streams

    Chúng Ta Của Tương Lai, a follow‑up single to Son Tung M‑TP’s EP Chúng Ta(2020), marked his return to the project after more than three years.

    It topped YouTube Vietnam’s trending chart, reached #1 on Spotify, iTunes, and YouTube Music in Vietnam. Although its global impact was more modest, it reaffirmed his strong local popularity and became one of the standout tracks from the EP.

  • June 8, 2024

    Đừng Làm Trái Tim Anh Đau
    (Don't Hurt My Heart)

    160M views | 60M streams

    Đừng Làm Trái Tim Anh Đau received positive reviews and became a major commercial success, debuting at #162 on the Billboard Global Excl. US chart, making it the second Vietnamese track to appear there after Muộn rồi mà sao còn.

    In Vietnam, the song topped streaming platforms and later peaked at #5, #7, and #17 on official charts.
