Controversies

What kind of controversies was Sơn Tùng M-TP Involved in?

Sơn Tùng M-TP is one of Vietnam's most influential pop artists, known for his distinctive sound, creativity, and ability to connect with audiences through both music and style. As a singer, songwriter, and producer, he has redefined what it means to be a V-pop star, blending modern pop and hip-hop influences with his own artistic vision. His music and image have inspired millions, shaping the sound and direction of Vietnamese pop culture while earning recognition from fans around the world.

Song Type Details Fan & Media Reaction
Cơn Mưa Ngang Qua Abandoning the underground culture When Son Tung rose quickly from indie tracks like Cơn mưa ngang qua to mainstream stardom, many underground rappers accused him of abandoning the underground culture and “selling out” to pop. Underground rappers like Andree Right Hand, Rhymastic, and B Ray openly criticized Son Tung for copying styles from K‑pop and rap beats from the underground without giving credit. Some accused him of using ghost producers and presenting himself as a self‑made artist, which clashed with the underground ethos of authenticity. Fans embraced it as his debut breakthrough; media praised his fresh style.
Nắng Ấm Xa Dần Plagiarism accusations Critics noted similarities to Korean ballads. Fans defended him as inspired by K‑pop; media raised originality concerns.
Em Của Ngày Hôm Qua Plagiarism accusations. Compared to G‑Dragon’s style, fueling debates about originality. Fans celebrated its popularity; critics highlighted “Vietnam’s G‑Dragon” debate.
Chắc Ai Đó Sẽ Về Plagiarism & ban Accused of copying Jung Yong‑hwa’s - Because I Miss You; temporarily banned by Ministry of Culture. Fans protested the ban; media widely covered it as a major scandal.
Chúng Ta Không Thuộc Về Nhau Plagiarism & ban Compared to Charlie Puth’s We Don’t Talk Anymore. Fans were split between defense and criticism as both songs debuted on the same day.; media reignited plagiarism debates
Lạc Trôi Cultural debate Fusion of áo dài with sneakers sparked discussion about tradition vs modernity. Fans loved the creativity; media debated respect for tradition.
Nơi Này Có Anh Plagiarism & ban Accused of copying DJ Genericname - Dear You Fans defended Son Tung, saying similarities were coincidental; media noted pattern of borrowing and questioned originality.
Chúng Ta Của Hiện Tại Copyright dispute MV removed from YouTube after plagiarism claims (KnowKnow, GC). Fans defended him; media highlighted copyright issues and industry oversight.
There's No One at All Legal ban & fine MV deemed harmful; removed from YouTube Vietnam, company fined 70M VND (~$2,656). Fans shocked and divided; media covered it as a national controversy.
